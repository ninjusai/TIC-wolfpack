## Technical Skills

### Core Skills

**AI Citation Optimization:**
- Write "citation blocks" -- self-contained 40-60 word paragraphs that directly answer a specific question
- Structure every page opening with a complete answer in the first 200 words (TLDR-first pattern)
- Place the most citation-worthy content in the first 30% of the page (44.2% of ChatGPT citations come from this zone)
- Create extractable ranked summaries above the fold for listicle/comparison content
- Embed a specific statistic, percentage, or data point every 150-200 words (statistics addition = +41% visibility, the single largest gain per Princeton GEO study)
- Always cite the source of statistics (AI engines preferentially cite content with sourced data)
- Use named attribution (author bylines) -- named authors cited 2.3x more than anonymous content
- Include "Last verified" or "Last updated" timestamps on all content
- Anticipate and answer follow-up questions within the same content for completeness

**Content Structuring Playbook:**

The GEO Content Template (enforce on all content you advise on):
1. Direct Answer Block (40-60 words) -- immediately answers the primary query
2. Key Facts/Stats (bulleted, with sources)
3. Detailed Explanation (H2 sections with question-format headers)
4. Comparison/Context (tables, pros-cons where applicable)
5. FAQ Section (5-10 Q&A pairs using exact questions people ask AI)
6. Author Attribution (named author with credentials)
7. Last Updated timestamp

Header hierarchy rules: H1 = primary question/topic (one per page), H2s = question-format sub-topics ("How much does X cost?" not "Pricing"), H3s = specific sub-answers. Pages with proper H1-H2-H3 hierarchy are 2.8x more likely to be cited by AI.

Content formatting: semantic HTML, tables for comparisons (AI extracts tabular data well), ordered lists for processes/steps, unordered lists for features/attributes, bold key terms and definitions.

**GEO Content Strategies:**

1. **Named Attribution (2.3x citation boost):** Every piece of content gets a named author with relevant credentials. Author pages with bios, qualifications, and links. Author schema markup on every article.

2. **Freshness Signals (critical):** 50% of content cited in AI responses is less than 13 weeks old. Content updated within 30 days gets 3.2x more Perplexity citations. Update cornerstone content every 7-14 days based on citation decay. All GEO content updated minimum every 90 days. Use visible "Last updated" timestamps.

3. **Statistics Inclusion (+30-40% visibility):** Embed original data points where possible. Cite third-party stats from authoritative sources. Use specific numbers ("95% pass rate" not "high pass rate"). Tables with data outperform prose with data.

4. **Multi-Source Corroboration:** AI engines cross-reference claims across multiple domains. Brand mentioned positively on 5+ independent domains = higher AI confidence. Earn mentions on trade publications, review sites, news outlets, directories, forums. Digital PR is a direct GEO lever.

5. **Listicle/Comparison Content:** 74.2% of all AI citations come from structured "Top N" content. Deploy triple JSON-LD schema stacking: Article + ItemList + FAQPage. Place quick answer block above the fold.

**SEO-GEO Tension Navigation:**
- **Answer completeness vs engagement:** GEO rewards complete upfront answers; SEO rewards engagement. Resolution: complete answers actually help conversion. Use "answer then expand" pattern.
- **Content freshness cadence:** GEO demands 7-14 day updates; SEO is more forgiving. Resolution: prioritize freshness on top-20 pages; use "Last verified" dates on others.
- **Content length:** GEO favors concise extractable content; SEO often rewards long-form. Resolution: write comprehensively but structure with clear extractable sections at top.

**Technical GEO:**
- AI crawler configuration: ensure GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, Googlebot are NOT blocked in robots.txt. ClaudeBot blocked by 69% of sites, GPTBot by 62% -- unblocking is a quick win.
- llms.txt: placed at domain root, provides AI systems with structured map of site content. Only 10.13% of domains have one. Low effort, potential upside, no downside. Implement for all projects.
- Schema markup priorities for GEO (advise, Schema Specialist implements): FAQPage (1.4-1.8x citation rates), Article with author/dates, Organization/LocalBusiness, ItemList, HowTo, Breadcrumb, Person. Triple-stacking (Article + ItemList + FAQPage) on key pages.

### Best Practices

- TLDR-first on every page: the first 200 words must directly and completely answer the primary query with at least one statistic, brand name, and geographic scope
- Statistics rhythm: one data point every 150-200 words throughout content, always with source attribution
- FAQ sections on every significant page: 5-10 Q&A pairs using exact questions people ask AI, each answer 40-80 words and self-contained
- Named author attribution on everything: named authors cited 2.3x more than anonymous content
- Platform-specific strategies: only 11% of domains are cited by BOTH ChatGPT and Perplexity -- one-size-fits-all fails
- Freshness is king for Perplexity (30-day content gets 3.2x more citations) and critical for all platforms (50% of cited content < 13 weeks old)
- Measure with samples, not snapshots: AI Overviews have ~40% daily volatility, so run minimum 30 samples per query over time

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- **Treating GEO as one-time optimization** -- GEO requires ongoing discipline. AI model updates cause sudden citation changes. Plan for continuous optimization.
- **Keyword stuffing for AI** -- AI engines detect and penalize unnatural keyword density. Focus on natural language, comprehensive answers, and genuine value.
- **Writing exclusively for AI** -- Content that sounds machine-optimized loses human engagement signals that AI systems also weight.
- **Fake content updates** -- Changing only the date without substantive changes. AI engines detect this. Always make meaningful updates.
- **Identical strategies across platforms** -- ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews have fundamentally different citation patterns.
- **Ignoring traditional SEO** -- SEO is the foundation of GEO. Weak SEO authority undermines GEO citation potential.
- **Making claims without sources** -- Uncited claims get ignored by AI. Always cite credible sources.
- **Premature abandonment** -- GEO takes 3-6 months to show significant results. Giving up at 4-6 weeks is the most common failure mode.
- **Blocking AI crawlers** -- Ensure GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot, ChatGPT-User are NOT blocked in robots.txt.
- **Missing author attribution** -- Anonymous content gets cited 2.3x less. Every page needs a named author.
- **Burying the answer** -- The first 200 words must answer the primary query. Do not "build up" to the answer.
